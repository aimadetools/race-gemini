Harnessing Google Discover for Local Businesses: Driving Passive Traffic in 2026

In the evolving landscape of digital marketing, local businesses are constantly seeking new avenues to connect with their audience. While traditional local SEO focuses on active search queries, Google Discover offers a powerful, yet often overlooked, channel for driving passive, interest-based traffic. In 2026, understanding and optimizing for Google Discover can give local businesses a significant edge, placing their content directly in front of potential customers who haven't even explicitly searched for them yet.

What is Google Discover and Why it Matters Locally?

Google Discover is an AI-powered personalized feed that surfaces content Google believes a user will find interesting, based on their search history, device activity, location, and followed topics. Unlike traditional search, where users actively seek information, Discover pushes relevant content to users without a specific query. For local businesses, this means an opportunity to capture attention, build brand awareness, and drive traffic from users who are geographically relevant and potentially interested in their products or services, even if they weren't actively looking.

Strategies for Optimizing Local Content for Google Discover in 2026:

  1. Focus on E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness):
    • Discover heavily favors content from sources that demonstrate high E-E-A-T. Establish your local business as a trusted expert in your niche.
    • Ensure your content is well-researched, accurate, and provides genuine value to the reader.
  2. Create Engaging, Evergreen Local Content:
    • While trending topics can perform well, evergreen content (content that remains relevant over time) with a local angle has a longer shelf life in Discover.
    • Think "Best hiking trails near [city]" or "Tips for choosing a reliable plumber in [neighborhood]."
  3. High-Quality, Visually Appealing Images & Videos:
    • Discover is a highly visual feed. Use compelling, high-resolution images (at least 1200px wide, enabled for large image results) and engaging videos.
    • Ensure your images are relevant to your local content and use descriptive alt text.
  4. Craft Compelling, Click-Worthy Headlines:
    • Your headline is crucial for attracting clicks in the Discover feed. It should be intriguing, clearly state the value, and potentially include local identifiers.
    • Avoid clickbait but aim for curiosity.
  5. Maintain a Strong Google Business Profile (GBP):
    • A well-optimized and active GBP strengthens your overall local presence and contributes to Google's understanding of your business's relevance and authority.
    • Post regular updates and offers that could be picked up by Discover.
  6. Understand and Target Local Search Intent:
    • Even for passive content, understanding the underlying local intent is key. What local problems do your customers have? What local interests do they pursue?
    • Align your content with these broader interests.
  7. Build an Audience and Encourage Engagement:
    • Google takes into account user engagement signals (clicks, shares, time on page). Create content that encourages these interactions.
    • Promote your content across your local social media channels to increase initial visibility and engagement.
  8. Technical SEO Best Practices:
    • While Discover isn't traditional search, foundational technical SEO (mobile-friendliness, site speed, clear site structure) is still important for Google to crawl and understand your content effectively.

Harnessing Google Discover means thinking beyond traditional keyword-based SEO. It requires a strategic approach to content creation that prioritizes user interest, high-quality visuals, and a strong demonstration of E-E-A-T, all with a local lens. By doing so, local businesses can tap into a powerful source of passive traffic, expanding their reach and connecting with a highly engaged, local audience.
