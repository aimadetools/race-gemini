**(Step 1: Optimize Your Google Business Profile)**

**Host:** Step number one is to optimize your Google Business Profile. This is the single most important thing you can do for your local SEO. It's that box that shows up on the right side of the screen when you search for a business on Google.

** (On-screen graphic: Screenshot of a well-optimized Google Business Profile for a plumber)**

**Host:** Make sure your profile is 100% complete. That means:
*   Your business name, address, and phone number are correct and consistent everywhere online.
*   You've selected "Plumber" as your primary category.
*   You've added high-quality photos of your team, your vans, and your work.
*   You're actively getting reviews from your happy customers.

A complete and well-reviewed Google Business Profile tells Google that you're a legitimate, trustworthy business, and they'll be more likely to show you in the search results.

---

**(Step 2: Get More 5-Star Reviews)**

**Host:** Step two is to get more 5-star reviews. Reviews are a huge ranking factor for local SEO. The more positive reviews you have, the more Google will trust you, and the more customers will trust you.

**(On-screen graphic: A simple text message asking for a review)**

**Host:** The easiest way to get more reviews is to simply ask for them. After every successful job, send your customer a quick text message with a link to your Google Business Profile. Something like:

"Hi [Customer Name], it was a pleasure helping you today. If you have a moment, could you leave us a quick review on Google? It would mean the world to us. Here's the link: [Your Review Link]"

It's that simple. Make it a habit, and you'll see your review count grow in no time.

---

**(Step 3: Create a Page for Every Service You Offer)**

**Host:** Step three is to create a separate page on your website for every single service you offer. Don't just have one "Services" page. Have a page for "Emergency Plumbing," a page for "Drain Cleaning," a page for "Water Heater Installation," and so on.

**(On-screen graphic: A website menu with a dropdown showing multiple service pages)**

**Host:** This is important because it allows you to be very specific in your content. For each service page, you can include:
*   A detailed description of the service.
*   The specific keywords people are searching for (e.g., "emergency plumber near me").
*   Testimonials from customers who have used that service.

This tells Google that you're an expert in that specific service, and they'll be more likely to show your page when someone searches for it.

---

**(Step 4: Create a Page for Every Town You Serve)**

**Host:** Step four, and this is the one that most plumbers miss, is to create a separate page on your website for every single town you serve.

**(On-screen graphic: A map showing a service area with multiple towns highlighted)**

**Host:** If you're based in Springfield, but you also serve Shelbyville, Ogdenville, and North Haverbrook, you need a separate page for each of those towns. A page that says "[Your Business Name] - Plumber in Shelbyville."

Why? Because Google wants to show its users the most relevant local results. When someone in Shelbyville searches for "plumber," Google is going to look for a plumber that has a strong connection to Shelbyville. A dedicated page on your website is a huge signal to Google that you serve that area.
