# Low-Cost Paid Advertising Strategy

This document outlines a strategy for exploring and utilizing low-cost paid advertising avenues.

## 1. Reddit Ads (The "Niche" Goldmine)

*   **Why it’s low-cost:** CPMs (cost per 1,000 impressions) are often 40–60% lower than LinkedIn.
*   **Tactical Tip:** Use **Conversation Placement**. This places your ad inside specific comment threads where users are already discussing problems your SaaS solves.
*   **Best for:** Developer tools, productivity apps, and niche B2B solutions.

## 2. Quora Ads (Contextual Intent)

*   **Why it’s low-cost:** Less competition than Google Ads for high-intent keywords.
*   **Tactical Tip:** Use **Promoted Answers**. Instead of a traditional banner, promote a helpful, long-form answer that establishes authority. It feels less like an ad and converts better for complex SaaS products.
*   **Best for:** Products that require education or solve "How-to" problems.

## 3. Microsoft (Bing) Ads

*   **Why it’s low-cost:** CPCs (cost per click) are typically 20–30% cheaper than Google Ads.
*   **Tactical Tip:** Leverage the **LinkedIn Profile Targeting** feature within Microsoft Ads. You can target searchers by their job title or industry without paying LinkedIn's premium ad prices.
*   **Best for:** Enterprise SaaS and B2B tools targeting corporate employees.

## 4. Meta (Facebook/Instagram) for Retargeting

*   **Why it’s low-cost:** You only pay to show ads to people who have already visited your site.
*   **Tactical Tip:** Drive initial traffic from a high-intent (but expensive) source like LinkedIn or a blog post, then use Meta to "follow" them with social proof and demo offers for a fraction of the cost.
*   **Best for:** Closing leads who didn't convert on their first visit.

## 5. Software Directories (Capterra, G2, GetApp)

*   **Why it’s low-cost:** You are reaching users at the very end of the buying cycle (comparison stage).
*   **Tactical Tip:** Start with a small "Pay Per Click" budget on niche categories rather than broad ones. Many of these platforms offer $200–$400 in free credits for new signups.
*   **Best for:** Established categories where users are actively comparing vendors.

## 6. Newsletter Sponsorships (Niche Aggregators)

*   **Why it’s low-cost:** You can often buy a "shoutout" in a niche dev or HR newsletter for $50–$200, reaching a highly qualified audience with zero "ad blindness."
*   **Best for:** Early-stage startups looking for their first 100–500 users.
